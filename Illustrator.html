Graphic Design

This logo was designed for my final project for my Interactive Design course during my junior year. I was designing a website for a coffee shop and wanted to create the logo for it as well. I used Illustrator to design this coffee mug with latte art.

This logo was also for the same final website project. I wanted to create various options to see which style I preferred for my website. I used Illustrator to design this as well and focused on layering circles.

This graphic was another graphic I designed for JMU's newspaper, "The Breeze". The article focused on how users compare themselves to the unrealistic standards social media stars promote. I decided to use a popular Tik-Tok star, Charli D'Amelio to demonstrate this message and a thought bubble from the user showing their envy. I used Illustrator to create this.

This graphic was designed for a UX design class I took the second semester of my junior year of college. In the beginning stages of design, we brainstormed three problems that people today struggle with. I came up with the idea of sustainability and designed the initial idea of a web browser that would rate each clothing store based on sustainability. I then created this design to demonstrate my idea to the class.

This logo was designed my 2nd year of college in a graphic design course. We were assigned to design a logo using shapes, fill, and punch out. I decided to re-design my dance studio's logo and used Adobe Illustrator to accomplish this. For this project we had to present it to the class on a poster containing this logo, the logo in different colors, and the logo on various products. For the products I used a Cricut to put the design on a shirt and a little bag.

This graphic was the first graphic I designed for JMU's newspaper, "The Breeze". The article focused on the impact social media has on mental health.
